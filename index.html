For Buyers: 

1. Real estate contracts are lengthy and dull. Often People do not read the whole contract. But understanding the hidden clauses is important.

2. Traditionally people have made a 20% down payment while buying a home. No one is denying that this has its own advantage of lower mortgage payment but if you are not in a situation to spend that money, you can avail private mortgage insurance.

3. Professional home inspectors also do mistakes. So instead of completely depending on them do self-inspection to the best of your ability.

4. We don’t get everything we want in life. For home buying also, do not reject a home right away just because it does not have a thing of your choice. Evaluate if you can build it later on and negotiate the price of a home.

For Sellers:

1. Setting the correct price for your home is important. In the post-pandemic world, people are meticulous about spending their hard-earned money. If the price tag on your home is not competitive enough, your property might just get overlooked.

2. Don’t just concentrate on your home’s interior. Do deck up the surroundings. Good surroundings can attract buyers.

3. Take care of small repairing works in your home before opening your doors for buyers. Unfinished improvements work here and there get spotted in inspection and provide space for negotiation with the buyer.

4. Get your inspection work done before you list your home. This reduces your chances to get caught off guard. This also reduces the chances of potential issues that may arise during the final deal.
